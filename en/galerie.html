What was done

Pinus parviflora: from dense mass to readable form

A real before/after pair: the same white pine work, made calmer and easier to read.

What was done
Dense areas were opened, distracting shoots were removed selectively and the cloud structure was restored.
Why
Light and air can move through the crown again, so the result looks cared for, not roughly clipped.
Viktor's value
Viktor's value is in reading the structure and keeping what the tree needs to grow well over the next years.
What was done

Low crown beside the terrace

Pinus parviflora with a low crown: growth control where the tree lives beside the client's terrace.

What was done
The lower mass was calmed, the silhouette opened and growth that crowded the terrace was reduced.
Why
The tree should remain part of the garden without blocking view, light or movement.
Viktor's value
Viktor works not only on the tree, but on how the tree fits its exact place.
What was done

Watereri: restoring the cloud structure

The clouds stay natural, while the spacing and direction become clear again.

What was done
The cloud pads were balanced, gaps were opened and the pine's natural rhythm was kept.
Why
Without air inside the crown, the shape quickly becomes heavy and loses proportion.
Viktor's value
The value is selective hand work: not cutting everything flat, but preserving living architecture.
What was done

Taxus baccata: topiary without a hard wall

Yew holds shape well, but needs calm hand correction instead of a quick mechanical cut.

What was done
The pads were cleaned, the outline gathered and chaotic new growth removed.
Why
The form stays precise without looking like a roughly clipped hedge.
Viktor's value
Viktor brings the line back without taking away the tree's living surface.
