From simple cutting to Japanese tree art.

Before my journey to Japan, I mostly cut trees: correct the shape, reduce density, keep them smaller. In Kyoto in 2009, I began to understand Japanese tree care as a master craft: reading a tree as a living being, respecting its reaction and working with nature's laws.

Since then, niwaki has been my path. I do not promise miracles. I promise to work correctly, correct errors and shape for the next years, not only for the first impression.
