Mistakes and diagnosis

Old needles, moss and dense crowns: common mistakes.

Old needles, brown inner areas or moss on branches do not automatically mean disease. They are signals. I read the whole pattern: light, air, water, previous cuts and how long needles stay wet.

RHS sources treat moss, algae and lichens as generally harmless on bark. But damp, shaded and congested conditions can make them more visible. Plant disease sources also point to moisture, poor air movement and dense growth as risk factors for needle problems.

Old needles

Natural ageing is possible. Rapid thinning or heavy browning needs a closer check.

Moss and coating

The coating is often a symptom of local conditions, not the root cause.

Dense shell

Fast shearing can trap shade and moisture inside the crown.

Fungal risk

Air movement, dry timing and hygiene matter before a small issue becomes structural.
